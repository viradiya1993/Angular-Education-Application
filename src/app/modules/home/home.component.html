About Me

Elizabeth Leone, M.Ed She is passionate about making learning more equitable and attainable for all students, especially English language learners (ELL) with limited or interrupted formal education (SLIFE) and significant social and emotional traumas. Since 2016, she has been developing effective and innovative ESL strategies for academic readiness which simultaneously focus on language acquisition, content education, and 21st century skills in a way that keeps ELLs motivated to learn. The shift to remote learning has relied heavily on the use of technology, which has left many ELLs struggling and teachers struggling profoundly with their data tracking. This is how Elizabeth became interested in how teachers can collect and use accurate and tangible data to guide instruction and practices. Elizabeth has taught ESL for the past 5 years. Her current position is in the ESOL department at Jewett Street Elementary School in Manchester, New Hampshire. Prior to public education, Elizabeth worked for three years as a children’s director for a non-profit serving many refugee families.
